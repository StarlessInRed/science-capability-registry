## Patch Heat-Flux Proxy

`postprocess/patch_heat_flux_proxy_summary.csv` is generated by `paired_patch_owner_cell_series_resistance_proxy`.

For each configured interface, the postprocessor:

- reads `case/constant/<region>/polyMesh` patch faces and owner cells;
- pairs owner and neighbour patch faces by nearest face center;
- reads final `T` owner-cell values;
- uses solid `thermal_conductivity_W_m_K` directly;
- estimates fluid conductivity as `mu * Cp / Pr`;
- computes a paired face heat-flux proxy and integrated heat-rate proxy.

| role | bottomAir_to_heater W | topAir_to_heater W |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| baseline | -0.72433985 | -0.00050765 |
| heater temperature high | -0.90547737 | -0.00063537 |
| airflow high | -0.72433985 | -0.00051164 |
| mesh refinement | -0.59658666 | -0.00009451 |

The sign convention is owner-to-neighbour. Negative `bottomAir_to_heater` means the hotter heater drives the proxy heat rate toward bottomAir. The heater-temperature perturbation increases the magnitude, matching the expected stronger thermal driver. The mesh-refinement values are not a mesh-independence claim because the proxy depends on face pairing, owner-cell distance, and short-horizon fields.
